Risks & Headwinds

Watch These Factors

●

Declining Deliveries: Tesla delivered 1.64M vehicles in 2025, down 8.6% YoY, losing the EV sales crown to BYD (2.26M units). Q4 U.S. sales fell 15.6% as the $7,500 federal EV tax credit expired.

●

Intensifying Competition: BYD now offers entry-level EVs below $10,000. Waymo, Zoox, and Nvidia's DRIVE platform pose threats to robotaxi dominance. Chinese humanoid robot firms (Unitree, UBTech) targeting 5,000-10,000 units in 2026.

●

FSD Regulatory Uncertainty: Musk now says Tesla needs 10 billion miles (up from 6B) for safe unsupervised FSD. Fleet projected to cross that threshold around July 2026. EU and China data sovereignty laws could delay global rollout.

●

Valuation Stretch: TSLA trades at a P/E of ~400x with operating margins compressed from 10.8% to 5.8%. Morningstar considers the stock overvalued, with ROIC below the weighted average cost of capital.

●

CEO Distraction & Brand Risk: Musk's political involvement and attention split across xAI, SpaceX, and DOGE continues to generate consumer backlash, particularly in Europe where sales have declined. The SpaceX merger talks add further complexity.
