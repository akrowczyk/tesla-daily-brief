Tesla Q4 2025 Earnings Report Tomorrow After Close

Tesla reports Q4 earnings on Jan 28 with Wall Street expecting $23.3B in revenue and $0.45 EPS. Key focus areas include automotive margin stabilization, FSD revenue, and 2026 delivery guidance after two consecutive years of declining sales.

FSD

Tesla Kills FSD One-Time Purchase, Moves to $99/Month Subscription

Starting February 14, Tesla will shift FSD to a subscription-only model at $99/month, eliminating the $8,000 one-time purchase option. Musk warns price will rise as capabilities improve. Existing owners retain lifetime access but transfers end March 31.

Robotaxi

Musk: Robotaxis to Be "Widespread" in U.S. by End of 2026

Speaking at Davos, Musk said Tesla robotaxis will be widespread across the U.S. by year-end. Austin rides are now operating with no safety monitor on select vehicles, while the service has expanded to the San Francisco Bay Area.

Production

Tesla Loses EV Crown as 2025 Deliveries Fall 8.6% to 1.64M

Tesla reported a second straight annual sales decline, delivering 1.64M vehicles in 2025 versus 1.79M in 2024. BYD surpassed Tesla as the world's largest EV maker with 2.26M units sold, driven by the end of U.S. EV tax credits and intensifying global competition.

Risks & Headwinds

Watch These Factors

●

Declining Deliveries & Lost EV Crown: Two consecutive years of declining sales with Q4 deliveries down 16% YoY. BYD has surpassed Tesla as the world's largest EV maker. Morningstar forecasts further delivery declines through Q3 2026.

●

BYD & Global Competition: BYD sold 2.26M EVs in 2025 vs Tesla's 1.64M. Xiaomi, NIO, Kia, Hyundai, and VW all gaining ground. In Europe, Tesla sales fell 38.8% YoY while Chinese brands surged.

●

Margin Compression: Automotive gross margins (ex-credits) compressed to ~14.3%. Q3 showed ~12% revenue growth but ~40% operating income decline. Price cuts continue to erode profitability.

●

Extreme Valuation: Trading at P/E of ~291 with a $1.45T market cap. Consensus price target of ~$397 implies ~9% downside. Valuation relies on AI, robotaxi, and robotics promises, not current fundamentals.

●

Policy & Brand Headwinds: The $7,500 federal EV tax credit expired in Sept 2025, directly pressuring U.S. sales. CEO Musk's political activities have triggered consumer backlash, particularly in Europe where sales dropped sharply.
