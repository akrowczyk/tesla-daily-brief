Risks & Headwinds

Watch These Factors

●

Declining Deliveries & Lost #1 Spot: Tesla delivered 1.64M vehicles in 2025 (down 9% YoY), losing the world's largest EV maker title to BYD which sold 2.26M vehicles. U.S. sales fell 15.6% in Q4.

●

Intensifying Global Competition: BYD, Xiaomi, and NIO are reshaping the EV landscape with lower-cost models and faster refresh cycles. BYD registrations surged 252% in Germany and 800% in the UK while Tesla's fell nearly half.

●

EV Tax Credit Expiration: The federal $7,500 EV tax credit ended in September 2025, directly pressuring sales volume and pricing power. Analysts forecast an accelerated 15% delivery decline in 2026.

●

Stretched Valuation (P/E ~299): Tesla trades at a P/E ratio near 300x, pricing in significant AI and robotaxi execution. Any delays to Cybercab or Optimus timelines could trigger a sharp re-rating.

●

Musk Political Risk & Brand Backlash: Elon Musk's political activity continues to hurt the brand, particularly in Europe. His divided attention across Tesla, xAI, SpaceX, and politics raises execution concerns.
