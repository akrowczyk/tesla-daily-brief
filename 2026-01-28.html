Tesla Q4 Earnings Beat: EPS $0.50 vs. $0.45 Expected, Revenue $24.9B

Tesla Invests $2B in Musk's xAI, Kills Model S & Model X

Tesla disclosed a $2 billion investment in xAI's Series E round at a $230B valuation, despite shareholder opposition. Musk also announced the end of Model S and X production, with those lines repurposed for Optimus robot manufacturing.

Robotaxi

Robotaxi Expansion: Seven New U.S. Cities Targeted for H1 2026

Tesla plans to expand its robotaxi service from Austin to Dallas, Houston, Phoenix, Miami, Orlando, Tampa, and Las Vegas in the first half of 2026. Cybercab mass production is set to begin in April at Giga Texas.

FSD

FSD Shifts to Subscription-Only; 1.1M Active Subscribers

Musk announced the one-time purchase option for FSD is being eliminated in favor of the $99/month subscription model. Tesla now has 1.1 million active FSD subscribers. Autopilot lane centering was also removed to drive FSD adoption.

Risks & Headwinds

Watch These Factors

●

Declining Deliveries: Tesla delivered 1.63M vehicles in 2025, down 8.6% YoY. Q4 deliveries fell 15.6% to 418,227 units, missing estimates. BYD overtook Tesla as the world's top EV seller with 2.26M units.

●

Margin Compression: Net income plunged 61% in Q4. Operating expenses surged 39%. Capex guidance of ~$20B for 2026 is nearly double Wall Street estimates, pressuring near-term profitability.

●

xAI Governance Concerns: The $2B xAI investment was made despite a shareholder vote effectively rejecting it. Ongoing lawsuits allege Musk breached fiduciary duties by diverting Tesla resources to xAI.

●

Regulatory & FSD Hurdles: Unsupervised FSD requires state-by-state approval. NHTSA safety probes ongoing. Cybercab may be forced to add steering wheel and pedals if regulations require manual control capability.

●

Brand & Political Risk: Musk's political activities continue to impact European sales and brand perception. EU sales fell sharply, with Germany and UK registrations declining as backlash against Musk's political engagement grows.
